Linus Torvalds is a Finnish-American software engineer best known as the creator and principal developer of the Linux kernel, which is the foundation of countless operating systems used worldwide. He is also the creator of Git, a widely used version control system. Torvalds's contributions have revolutionized the software industry, fostering innovation in open-source development and enabling collaboration across the globe.
